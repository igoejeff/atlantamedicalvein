Understanding Vein Disease

What Are Varicose Veins and Spider Veins?

Clinical information to help patients understand their condition before their first visit.

Varicose Veins

Varicose veins are enlarged, twisted veins that appear predominantly in the legs and feet. They develop when the one-way valves inside veins weaken or fail, allowing blood to pool and causing the vein wall to stretch and bulge. Varicose veins are a clinical manifestation of chronic venous insufficiency (CVI).

Common symptoms include:

  • Aching, heaviness, or fatigue in the legs
  • Visible bulging, rope-like veins beneath the skin
  • Swelling in the lower legs and ankles
  • Skin discoloration or thickening near the ankle
  • Cramping or restless legs, especially at night

Spider Veins

Spider veins (telangiectasias) are small, dilated blood vessels visible near the surface of the skin, typically appearing as red, blue, or purple web-like patterns. Unlike varicose veins, they do not bulge and are generally smaller. They commonly appear on the legs and face.

Spider veins may indicate:

  • Underlying venous insufficiency feeding the surface veins
  • Hormonal changes (pregnancy, birth control, menopause)
  • Prolonged standing or sedentary work
  • Genetic predisposition to vein weakness
  • Sun exposure (facial spider veins)

Treatment Options

Three Clinically Proven Procedures

CITY_NAME Medical Clinic offers exactly three in-office vein procedures. The appropriate treatment is selected based on vein size, location, ultrasound findings, and clinical presentation. All procedures are minimally invasive, require no hospital admission, and take 5–15 minutes.

01 — Thermal / Energy Ablation

Endovenous Ablation

Endovenous ablation is a minimally invasive thermal procedure for treating larger, symptomatic varicose veins. A thin catheter is inserted into the diseased vein under ultrasound guidance and local anesthesia. Heat energy is applied through the catheter to close the vein from the inside. No surgical incisions are required.

02 — FDA-Approved Foam Sclerotherapy

Varithena® Microfoam

Varithena® (polidocanol injectable foam) is an FDA-approved microfoam sclerotherapy agent used to treat incompetent saphenous veins and suitable varicose vein treatments. The microfoam displaces blood within the vein and causes the vessel wall to collapse and be reabsorbed by the body over time.

03 — Liquid Injection

Sclerotherapy

Sclerotherapy is the gold-standard in-office treatment for spider veins and small varicosities. A sclerosant solution is injected directly into the target vessel using a fine needle. The chemical irritates the vessel wall, causing it to seal shut and gradually fade. No anesthesia required.
