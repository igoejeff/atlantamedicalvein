Treatment Options

Three Proven Procedures

We use the appropriate procedure based on vein size, location, and clinical findings. Your treatment plan is determined after ultrasound evaluation.

01 — Thermal Ablation
Endovenous Ablation

A thermal or energy-based procedure used to treat larger, symptomatic varicose veins. A thin catheter is inserted into the diseased vein under ultrasound guidance. The vein is then closed using heat energy. Performed in-office under local anesthesia with no surgical incisions.

For Larger Varicose Veins
02 — Foam Sclerotherapy
Varithena®

Varithena is an FDA-approved injectable microfoam used for suitable varicose vein treatments. The microfoam is delivered directly into the diseased vein, causing it to collapse and be absorbed by the body. Administered in-office without anesthesia.

FDA-Approved Microfoam
03 — Sclerotherapy
Sclerotherapy

A liquid sclerosant solution is injected directly into spider veins and small varicosities. The solution irritates the vessel wall, causing it to close and fade over time. Fast, in-office procedure requiring no anesthesia and no downtime.

Spider Veins & Small Varicosities
What to Expect

Fast, In-Office. Minimal Downtime.

Every visit follows a clear, consistent process. No surprises, no hospital stays.

1
Consultation & Ultrasound

Your first visit includes a clinical consultation and a venous duplex ultrasound to map your veins and identify diseased vessels. Ultrasound screening is not complimentary.

2
Personalized Treatment Plan

Based on your ultrasound findings and clinical evaluation, your specialist creates a custom treatment plan. Insurance patients begin the prior authorization process before procedures are scheduled.

3
In-Office Procedure

Procedures are performed in-office, typically in 5–15 minutes. No hospital. No general anesthesia. Cash-pay patients may be treated the same day as their evaluation.

4
Walk Out & Resume Activity

Most patients walk out immediately after their procedure. Downtime is minimal. Your specialist will provide specific post-procedure instructions at the time of your visit.

Insurance Patients: All insurance-covered procedures require prior authorization. Same-day procedures are available for cash-pay patients only. Our team will verify your benefits and manage the authorization process on your behalf.
